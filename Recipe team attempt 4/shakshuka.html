Shakshuka, where all roads lead to goodness

Get ready for an exciting new recipe that will tantalize your taste buds!

A sharp knife is your best friend when cutting onions and peppers, if you are serious about getting the thinnest onions and peppers, consider learning about knife sharpening. This act will soothe the soul, and (somewhat) reduce the cooking time of this dish.

In case you have any hesitations, a sharp knife is a kitchen safety tool, since most experienced chefs will tell you that the most serious injuries they saw was with a not so sharp knife that slips!!!!

Ingredients

  • Extra-virgin olive oil 3 tablespoons
  • Onion 1 large, halved and thinly sliced
  • Red bell pepper 1 large, seeded and thinly sliced
  • Garlic cloves 3, thinly sliced
  • Ground cumin 1 teaspoon
  • Sweet paprika 1 teaspoon
  • Ground cayenne ⅛ teaspoon, or to taste
  • Whole plum tomatoes 1 (28-ounce) can, coarsely chopped with juices
  • Kosher salt ¾ teaspoon, plus more as needed
  • Black pepper ¼ teaspoon, plus more as needed
  • Feta 5 ounces, crumbled (about 1¼ cups)
  • Large eggs 6
  • Chopped cilantro For serving
  • Hot sauce For serving

Instructions

  1. Heat oven to 375 degrees.
  2. Heat oil in a large skillet over medium-low.
  3. Add onion and bell pepper. Cook gently until very soft, about 20 minutes.
  4. Add garlic and cook until tender, 1 to 2 minutes.
  5. Stir in cumin, paprika, and cayenne, and cook for 1 minute.
  6. Pour in tomatoes and season with ¾ teaspoon salt and ¼ teaspoon pepper. Simmer until tomatoes have thickened, about 10 minutes.
  7. Taste and add more salt and pepper if needed.
  8. Stir in crumbled feta.
  9. Gently crack eggs into skillet over tomatoes. Season eggs with salt and pepper.
  10. Transfer skillet to oven and bake until eggs are just set, 7 to 10 minutes.
  11. Sprinkle with cilantro and serve with hot sauce.
